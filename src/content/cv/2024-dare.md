Staff Engineer and web team lead on Dare's core ETRM (Energy Trading and Risk Management) platform — a complex, real-time system traders depend on daily, built on React, WebSockets, and NATS messaging.

I built a **new AI product surface for traders**, shipped as its own micro frontend. It paired an MCP-backed LLM chat for querying live trade data in natural language with generative UI for displaying and interacting with trades. The chat worked end-to-end but proved too slow for traders' tempo, so I shelved it and pivoted the effort into the MCP-driven generative UI, which fed directly into later work.

I led a **consolidated real-time trade feed** that replaced watching each exchange in its own app. We normalised heterogeneous feeds (multicast and Google Pub/Sub) into one canonical model over NATS / JetStream, added capabilities the native tickers lacked (e.g. historical replay), and rolled it out desk by desk starting with the CEO's. It won on adoption against the exchanges' own tools.

I built the **company's component library** from the ground up on Tailwind and shadcn/ui, replacing four coexisting styling systems (Sass, Ant Design, MUI, styled-components). It included trading-specific components and layouts, and became the consistency backbone across every micro frontend.

I also led the micro-frontend migration of the 8-year-old monolithic frontend (RSBuild), shrinking deployment blast radius from the whole app to a single section and letting the team ship faster and more safely — and led development on the Electron desktop application while championing stronger testing (Vitest, Playwright) and CI/CD across the team.
