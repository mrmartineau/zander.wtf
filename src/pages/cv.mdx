Product engineer with 18+ years building for the web — front-end at my core,
comfortable across the full stack. I focus on shipping the right thing: understanding
the user, shaping the product, and owning the outcome, not just the ticket. My work
spans fintech, health, music, and energy trading, usually on complex, data-heavy
applications where design systems and interface quality have outsized impact.

At Dare I was Staff Engineer and web team lead on mission-critical ETRM software. I
led a monolith-to-micro-frontend migration, built the component library that held it
together, and built a new AI product surface for traders: an MCP-backed LLM chat for
querying live trade data in natural language. When the chat proved too slow for the
tempo traders work at, I shelved it and channelled the effort into MCP-driven
generative UI, which is where the lasting value landed. That's the pattern I bring to AI:
build it seriously, judge it on real use, keep what works.

I'm a firm believer in AI-assisted development. A deep understanding of the web
platform means I can direct and orchestrate AI agents well — accelerating the right
things without sacrificing quality. The engineers who get the most from these tools
know when to lean in and when to pull back; throwing too many concerns at an agent
at once is a fast path to technical debt.

I care about long-term impact on a team: writing maintainable code, raising
standards, and helping the people around me do their best work.
